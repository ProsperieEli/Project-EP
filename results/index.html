Why is this information useful?

Knowing what items to have in the event of a disaster is the same as knowing what
tools to have before working on a project. Being caught off-guard, or lacking in a
particular item could lead to serious consequences. That is why we wish to urge all to
ensure that they have 100% of the essential items, and at minimum 80% of the needed
items. These items are the most recommended to aid in the event of a disaster.

Storing & Caring

You now have your emergency kit made and ready for use. That's awesome! Only, one
issue: there isn't a situation where it is currently needed. Hopefully, there should
never be a situation you find yourself in where you'll need it, but disasters
can always occur. Which is why you also need to be familiar with how to care for and store these
useful kits in preparation for if the time ever does arise.

Suggested items still needed :

The items you've put together in this emergency kit are items that could save your, and another
person's life one day. So keeping each item in top condition should be a top concern. Keeping no food
that can spoil is important, but also ensuring that you periodically check to ensure nothing ever truly
goes bad in terms of food. Ensure no dents form in any of the cans and no signs of entry are visible. Outside
of food items, make it a habit to ensure all items are fresh and working(ex: medication up to date).
Keep all the items in an area that can be easily accessed in the event of a disaster. While most disasters
are broadcasted along the news of their incoming threat, some can spring at any moments notice. Which means you
should have this kit somewhere accessible at the drop of a hat.
